Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about using the MelanoDetect screening interface and interpreting its AI-driven outputs.

Is this a medical diagnosis?

No. This is a screening tool. Its outputs are intended for review by medical professionals and should always be validated by a clinical dermatologist.

What images work best?

High-resolution, focused, and well-lit images of single lesions provide the most reliable results. Macro photography of skin lesions is recommended.

What is the "Attention Map"?

The attention map (Grad-CAM) highlights the areas of the image that the model focused on when making its prediction. It helps users understand why the model chose a specific class.

Privacy and Data Security

All processing is done locally on your machine. Your images are processed in memory and are not uploaded to any server or stored in any database.

Technical Specifications

The app uses a Deep Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) trained on thousands of dermatoscopic images to classify lesions into 7 supported categories.

System Limitations

Like all AI systems, MelanoDetect may have false positives or negatives. It is designed to assist, not replace, professional medical judgement.
